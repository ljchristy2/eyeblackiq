The Model Pipeline

Step 1 — Strength Ratings

Two independent rating systems are computed for every team:

  • ELO — Elo rating updated after every game. Head-to-head win probability uses P = 1 / (1 + 10^(-Δ/400)). Home field advantage = +25 ELO pts.
  • ISR — Iterative Strength Rating converges to a stable power-ranking via repeated margin-of-victory regression. Updated weekly.

Blend: 60% ELO + 40% ISR. The gap between ELO and ISR directly drives the confidence level.

Step 2 — Market Edge

Model probability is compared to the no-vig (fair) market probability.

Devig method: imp = abs(o)/(abs(o)+100) then normalize two-way to 100%.

Edge = Model% − Market%

Only positive edge signals advance to tier assignment. The market isn't always wrong — the model only acts when there's a measurable discrepancy.

Step 3 — SP ERA Adjustment (Baseball)

Starting pitcher quality shifts team win probability based on ERA vs. league average (5.70).

Formula: r = 5.0 × SP_ERA / 5.70 (capped 1–9 runs)

ERA adjustment: (r_away − r_home) / 20 added to home win probability.

A dominant home SP (ERA 2.50) vs. shaky away SP (ERA 6.20) can shift p_home by ~3-4pp.

Step 4 — 5-Gate Filter

Every signal must pass all five gates in sequence. Any failure blocks the pick.

  1. Pythagorean gate — team performance vs. expected (GREEN or cleared YELLOW)
  2. Edge ≥ 2% above no-vig fair probability
  3. ELO and ISR models agree within 15pp
  4. Line has not moved >0.5 units sharp-direction against model
  5. ETL data timestamp < 4 hours old

Kelly Sizing

All units are sized using Quarter Kelly with a hard bankroll cap.

  • Kelly fraction: 0.25 (1/4 of full Kelly)
  • Hard cap: 3% of bankroll per play
  • Daily maximum: 15 plays
  • 1 unit = 1% of bankroll (e.g. 2.0u = 2% of bankroll)

Units are set by tier, not raw Kelly — the tier floor ensures minimum sizing when edge is confirmed. Kelly is used as a ceiling check only.

Confidence Levels

What Confidence Means — and Why Tier Comes First

Confidence is a grounded signal about how well-supported this particular signal is — not whether it's a good bet. It's built from objective inputs, not gut feel.

●●● HIGH — 5+ SP starts + ELO/ISR agree within 5pp
●●○ MED — 2-4 SP starts or 5-10pp model gap
●○○ LOW — TBD SP, <2 starts, or >10pp gap

Critical distinction: Tier (edge%) determines your stake. Confidence tells you how reliable the underlying model inputs are. A FILTHY pick at MED confidence still has 12%+ edge — that's a significantly larger expected value than a SCOUT at HIGH confidence with 2.4% edge.

Example: BYU +1400 FILTHY 2.0u LOW ●○○ (23.9% edge) vs. Auburn +105 WHEELHOUSE 1.5u HIGH ●●● (6.2% edge). BYU's edge is nearly 4× larger. LOW confidence signals that SP data is limited — not that the edge is fabricated. Auburn's HIGH confidence reflects strong model agreement and confirmed SP starts. Both are legitimate plays; size them accordingly.

POD Rule: Pick of the Day requires HIGH confidence + WHEELHOUSE or better. PODs are the face of the system — they should reflect our highest-conviction signals where both the edge AND the model certainty are strong.

Run Line / Spread Alternative

When ML > +250 — Consider the Spread

When the model finds significant edge on a big underdog (ML > +250), the system flags a run line alternative. Instead of betting BYU at +1400 ML (where you need them to actually win), you can bet BYU +1.5 runs at a much lower price (typically +300 to +500) and still cash if they keep it close.

This is especially valuable for teams like BYU where our model says 30% win probability but the market says 6% — the "surprise competitive game" outcome is more likely than outright victory. The run line captures value from the team being competitive without requiring an upset.

Future feature: When ML edge >15% on a big dog, the system will automatically surface the run line as a separate bet with its own edge calculation against the offered run line odds.

Risk Controls & Kill Switches

Automated Kill Switches
  • 3-month rolling CLV < 0 → full suspension
  • 25 consecutive losses → 72-hour review period
  • 7 consecutive losing days → stakes reduced to 25% for 5 days
Go-Live Standards

No model goes live until all four thresholds are cleared:

  • Backtest ROI > 3%
  • CLV (Closing Line Value) ≥ 55%
  • Calibration within ±2% across all probability bins
  • Minimum 300–500 sample plays in paper trading
Closing Line Value (CLV)

CLV measures whether we're beating the closing line — the final market price after sharp money has moved the line. Pinnacle closing line is our gold standard benchmark.

Beating the closing line at 55%+ rate is the strongest long-term profitability signal — more reliable than win/loss record over small samples.

Volume Philosophy & What the Model Doesn't Do

Volume Policy — No Caps

EyeBlackIQ posts all plays with measurable edge (EV ≥ 2%). There is no daily cap on number of plays. The market doesn't limit its inefficiencies to 5 per day — neither does the model.

The tier system handles sizing: T1 plays get 2.0u, T2 get 1.5u, T3 get 1.0u. Volume is managed through edge threshold, not an artificial daily quota.

Kill switches handle drawdown protection automatically (25-loss streak, 7 losing days, 3-month CLV negative) — not volume caps.

What the Model Does Not Do
  • It does not predict game scores directly — it computes win probability vs. market implied probability
  • It does not claim to be right every day — edge means winning over large samples, not game-by-game
  • It does not chase losses — kill switches trigger reduced sizing automatically
  • It does not post picks without data — both TBD starting pitchers blocks NCAA signal generation
  • It does not use subjective gut reads — every signal is fully model-derived
  • It does not guarantee future results — past edge calibration doesn't guarantee forward performance
Three Layers of Transparency
  • Signal layer: Every pick posted with tier, edge %, EV, confidence, and line at time of signal
  • Results layer: Every outcome recorded publicly — wins and losses, unit P&L per pick
  • Model layer: Full methodology published here — no black boxes, no cherry-picked records

The full market view section on the Results page shows every game analyzed — not just picks with positive edge. This lets you see what the model passed on and why.

POD System — Pick of the Day

Each sport gets at most one POD per day. POD selection algorithm: highest ev_pct × win_probability_gap with minimum 7% EV.

POD picks require HIGH confidence (●●●) plus WHEELHOUSE tier or better. This ensures the system's flagship pick represents both strong edge AND strong model certainty.

PODs are tracked separately in the Results page. This provides a clean comparison between the high-conviction signal and the full model record.

Pick'em Markets

Near-50/50 Games — How They're Flagged and Why

Picks with odds between -120 and +120 are labeled Pick'em throughout the site. These represent games where the market sees a near-50/50 split in win probability.

Pick'ems are not inherently more or less valuable than other picks. However, they serve a distinct informational purpose:

  • When our model also shows roughly equal probability, the pick'em badge is a calibration check — both model and market agree, which increases confidence in the no-vig probability estimate
  • When our model significantly disagrees with a pick'em line, the edge reading may be inflated relative to higher-priced plays — the badge surfaces this for manual review
  • Pick'ems are tracked separately in the Results page to allow independent calibration analysis

You can filter for Pick'em picks on both the Today and Results pages using the Pick'ems filter button.

Full Market View

All Games Analyzed — Not Just Recommended Picks

The Full Market View section (visible at the bottom of the Today page) shows every game the model processed that day — including games where edge fell below the 2% threshold or other gates were not met. These are informational outputs only with no unit sizing.

Why this matters:

  • Transparency — you can see what the model passed on and why, not just what it liked
  • False positives — occasionally a model outputs a low-confidence edge that doesn't survive full gate filtering; the Full Market View shows these without dressing them as recommendations
  • Long-term tracking — all Full Market View picks are graded in the Results page under the same methodology as recommended picks, allowing unbiased record-keeping across the full model output
