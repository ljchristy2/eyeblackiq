The Model Pipeline

Step 1 — Strength Ratings

Two independent rating systems are computed for every team:

  • ELO — Elo rating updated after every game. Head-to-head win probability uses P = 1 / (1 + 10^(-Δ/400)). Home field advantage = +25 ELO pts.
  • ISR — Iterative Strength Rating converges to a stable power-ranking via repeated margin-of-victory regression. Updated weekly.

Blend: 60% ELO + 40% ISR. The gap between ELO and ISR directly drives the confidence level.

Step 2 — Market Edge

Model probability is compared to the no-vig (fair) market probability.

Devig method: imp = abs(o)/(abs(o)+100) then normalize two-way to 100%.

Edge = Model% − Market%

Only positive edge signals advance to tier assignment. The market isn't always wrong — the model only acts when there's a measurable discrepancy.

Step 3 — SP ERA Adjustment (Baseball)

Starting pitcher quality shifts team win probability based on ERA vs. league average (5.70).

Formula: r = 5.0 × SP_ERA / 5.70 (capped 1–9 runs)

ERA adjustment: (r_away − r_home) / 20 added to home win probability.

A dominant home SP (ERA 2.50) vs. shaky away SP (ERA 6.20) can shift p_home by ~3-4pp.

Step 4 — 5-Gate Filter

Every signal must pass all five gates in sequence. Any failure blocks the pick.

  1. Pythagorean gate — team performance vs. expected (GREEN or cleared YELLOW)
  2. Edge ≥ 2% above no-vig fair probability
  3. ELO and ISR models agree within 15pp
  4. Line has not moved >0.5 units sharp-direction against model
  5. ETL data timestamp < 4 hours old

Kelly Sizing

All units are sized using Quarter Kelly with a hard bankroll cap.

  • Kelly fraction: 0.25 (1/4 of full Kelly)
  • Hard cap: 3% of bankroll per bet
  • Daily maximum: 15 bets
  • 1 unit = 1% of bankroll (e.g. 2.0u = 2% of bankroll)

Units are set by tier, not raw Kelly — the tier floor ensures minimum sizing when edge is confirmed. Kelly is used as a ceiling check only.

Confidence Levels

What Confidence Means — and Why Tier Comes First

Confidence is a grounded signal about how well-supported this particular signal is — not whether it's a good bet. It's built from objective inputs, not gut feel.

●●● HIGH — 5+ SP starts + ELO/ISR agree within 5pp
●●○ MED — 2-4 SP starts or 5-10pp model gap
●○○ LOW — TBD SP, <2 starts, or >10pp gap

Critical distinction: Tier (edge%) determines your stake. Confidence tells you how reliable the underlying model inputs are. A FILTHY pick at MED confidence still has 12%+ edge — that's a significantly larger expected value than a SCOUT at HIGH confidence with 2.4% edge.

Example: BYU +1400 FILTHY 2.0u LOW ●○○ (23.9% edge) vs. Auburn +105 WHEELHOUSE 1.5u HIGH ●●● (6.2% edge). BYU's edge is nearly 4× larger. LOW confidence signals that SP data is limited — not that the edge is fabricated. Auburn's HIGH confidence reflects strong model agreement and confirmed SP starts. Both are legitimate bets; stake them accordingly.

POD Rule: Pick of the Day requires HIGH confidence + WHEELHOUSE or better. PODs are the face of the system — they should reflect our highest-conviction signals where both the edge AND the model certainty are strong.

Run Line / Spread Alternative

When ML > +250 — Consider the Spread

When the model finds significant edge on a big underdog (ML > +250), the system flags a run line alternative. Instead of betting BYU at +1400 ML (where you need them to actually win), you can bet BYU +1.5 runs at a much lower price (typically +300 to +500) and still cash if they keep it close.

This is especially valuable for teams like BYU where our model says 30% win probability but the market says 6% — the "surprise competitive game" outcome is more likely than outright victory. The run line captures value from the team being competitive without requiring an upset.

Future feature: When ML edge >15% on a big dog, the system will automatically surface the run line as a separate bet with its own edge calculation against the offered run line odds.

Go-Live Standards

No model goes live for real-money betting until all four thresholds are cleared:

  • Backtest ROI > 3%
  • CLV (Closing Line Value) ≥ 55%
  • Calibration within ±2% across all probability bins
  • Minimum 300–500 sample bets in paper trading
Closing Line Value (CLV)

CLV measures whether we're beating the closing line — the final market price after sharp money has moved the line. Pinnacle closing line is our gold standard benchmark.

Beating the closing line at 55%+ rate is the strongest long-term profitability signal — more reliable than win/loss record over small samples.
